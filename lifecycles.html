The operating model

Three lifecycles,
one connected person.

We don't serve one audience, we serve three: the customer who buys, the consultant who sells, and the creator who refers. Each needs its own lifecycle, but the real value is in connecting them, so a person's history and worth follow them as they move between roles. This is the model I've been building.

What it is

Three parallel lifecycles (customer, consultant, creator) that share one identity and one loyalty engine, so we manage the whole relationship, not three disconnected ones.

How I built it

Mapped every touchpoint and vendor per audience, unified them on a shared customer profile in our CDP, built the journeys in Customer.io, and ran the rewards through Talon.One.

Why it matters

A happy customer can become a seller. A creator drives new customers. When the lifecycles connect, each one makes the others stronger instead of competing for the same person.

The part that matters

How the three connect

Run in isolation, they compete for the same person. Connected, each one feeds the next.

Customer → Consultant

The best sellers often start as loyal customers. When someone hits the signals, the customer journey hands off to a recruit invite instead of just another promo.

Creator → Customer

Creators bring new customers in at the top of the funnel. Their referrals enter the customer lifecycle tagged to the creator, so we can credit and reward what converts.

Consultant ↑ Customer offers

When a consultant performs, their customers unlock better offers. The seller earns more, the customer spends more, and the two lifecycles reinforce each other.

Under all three
One identityA shared customer profile in the CDP ties a person's activity together, even as they play more than one role.
One loyalty engineTalon.One runs the points, tiers, and rewards across every audience instead of three separate systems.
One measurement layerAdobe Analytics and incrementality testing, so we know which connection actually drove value.
Why this maps to a cross-property loyalty program

This is the same problem a company with multiple properties has to solve: tie a person together across everything they touch, under one identity, so points and status follow them. I've been living this exact challenge, connecting three audiences across many vendors and platforms into one view. Different logos, identical problem.
